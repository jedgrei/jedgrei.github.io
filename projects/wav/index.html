Shenzhen Solitaire

Download (Windows only)

Shenzhen Solitaire is a solitaire variant created for the game Shenzhen I/O, by Zachtronics. I prefer it to Klondike solitiare (the "normal" version), mainly due to the larger number of winnable deals (99%!). It also just feels like it has more sustenance, instead of being driven only by randomness. So, I thought it would be worth trying to create it from scratch.

I made my version using SDL2 and C++, with Adobe Illustrator for the graphics. I think it turned out well (although I do have to clean it up a little bit). If you enjoyed it, you should check out the original and support the devs!


Instructions

Shenzhen Solitaire is fairly similar to FreeCell. Cards can be stacked descending (with alternating colors), and single cards can be put into the 3 cells at the top-right. Numbered cards can be placed from 1-9, by suit, in the foundations at the top-left. However, there are now 3 suits, all with seperate colors, and 4 dragons, which cannot be stacked on other cards. There's also a flower card, which clears itself. All 4 dragons of the same color can be removed into a cell by pressing their respective button when they are all uncovered. They need either a open cell, or a cell with one of the dragons in it. Once the dragons occupy a cell, no other cards can be placed into it.

If the foundations are full and the dragons have all been cleared, you win!
